Overview

Masai Mara Game Reserve:
Africa’s most popular safari destination, The Mara is home to the awesome black-manned lion, the elusive leopard, stately giraffes, vast herds of elephants, rhino, thundering herds of zebra, migrating wildebeest and numerous other plains game.
Masai Mara Game Reserve:
Africa’s most popular safari destination, The Mara is home to the awesome black-manned lion, the elusive leopard, stately giraffes, vast herds of elephants, rhino, thundering herds of zebra, migrating wildebeest and numerous other plains game.
Lake Nakuru National Park:
It is best known for its thousands, sometimes millions of flamingos nesting along the shores. you will enjoy the wide ecological diversity and varied habitats that range from Lake Nakuru itself to the surrounding escarpment and picturesque ridges
Amboseli National Park:
The name "Amboseli" comes from a Maasai word meaning "salty dust".
The park is found at the foot of Mount Kilimanjaro, it is one of the best places in Africa to view large herds of elephants up close.
Lake Manyara National Park:
You will see great birdlife along the shore, and location alone makes it a great place to spend a couple of nights. Also the meadows along the lake shores and forest are home to a wide variety of wildlife including leopard, hippos, a variety of monkeys, various birds, greater and lesser flamingos, storks, and pelicans. Several pleasant, private campsites are located in forest clearings by the lake
Ngorongoro Conservation Area:
The best place in Tanzania to see black rhino as well as prides of lion that include the magnificent black-manned males. There are lots of colorful flamingos and a variety of water birds. Other game that you can see includes leopard, cheetah, hyena, other members of the antelope family, and small mammals.
Serengeti National Park:
Placed adjacent next to Kenya's Massai Mara National Reserve. Serengeti amazing landscapes with its sprawling grasslands dotted with lone acacia trees and grazing herds of zebra and antelope is world heritage site, where earth’s largest concentration of plain game still roams free!
